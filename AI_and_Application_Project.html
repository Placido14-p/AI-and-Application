Introduction¶

The hotel industry has a background, including the significance of making data-driven decisions. Hotel business is very dynamic and competitive. Informed decisions are becoming more and more imperative in success in such an environment. The development of online booking systems and digital platforms has resulted in a data overload, and it is necessary to rely on data-driven strategies as a competitive benefit. This project is intended to use this data to gain actionable information on the hotel booking demand.

The aim of the project is to examine a detailed hotel reservation dataset in order to draw major trends and patterns. With the use of data science, the analysis will help show factors that drive booking demand, cancellation, and customer behavior. The insights would assist the managers of the hotels to maximize their prices, marketing, and resource allocation strategies.

Problem Statement¶

The need to analyze a comprehensive dataset of hotel booking that can be used to determine the key trends and patterns forms the problem statement of this project as it is stated in the Ai and Applications-IT_FINAL.docx file. The main problem is to bring data-supported information that may result in an improved business choice of hotel managers, like the pricing optimization, marketing, and resource distribution. The project will expose variables that affect the demand to make a booking, cancellation rate, and customer behavior.

def add_labels(ax, fmt="{:.0f}"):
    for p in ax.patches:
        height = p.get_height()
        if not np.isnan(height):
            ax.annotate(fmt.format(height),
                        (p.get_x() + p.get_width() / 2., height),
                        ha='center', va='bottom', fontsize=9, rotation=0)

Conclusion¶

The project has managed to investigate the dataset, and some major insights were obtained. As it has been analyzed, the overall cancellation rate was high and there was a significant variation in booking trends and cancellations between City Hotels and Resort Hotels. The data also indicated the significance of lead time, whereby an increased lead time is linked to an increased risk of cancellations.
